Essential Online Safety Tips

  1. Use strong and unique passwords
    Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password for multiple accounts.
  2. Do not share personal information online
    Never share details like your address, phone number, passwords, or banking information on public platforms.
  3. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
    Adding an extra verification step helps protect your account even if your password is compromised.
  4. Avoid clicking unknown or suspicious links
    Phishing links may look legitimate but can steal your data or install malware. Always check the sender before clicking.
  5. Keep your software updated
    Regular updates fix security vulnerabilities and protect your device from the latest threats.
  6. Use secure Wi-Fi connections
    Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities like online banking. Use a VPN when possible.
  7. Install antivirus and firewall protection
    Security software helps detect and block viruses, malware, and other threats.
  8. Log out from shared devices
    Always log out of your accounts when using public or shared computers.
