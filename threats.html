What are Cyber Threats?

Cyber threats are harmful activities carried out using computers, networks, or the internet. These threats aim to steal data, damage systems, or cause emotional and financial harm to users.

Common Types of Cyber Threats

1. Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks involve fake emails, messages, or websites that trick users into sharing personal information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details.

2. Malware and Viruses

Malware includes viruses, worms, spyware, and ransomware. These malicious programs can damage files, slow down devices, or steal sensitive data.

3. Identity Theft

Identity theft happens when attackers steal personal information and use it to impersonate someone, often for financial gain or illegal activities.

4. Online Fraud

Online fraud includes fake shopping websites, lottery scams, and investment scams designed to trick users into sending money.

5. Cyber Bullying

Cyber bullying involves harassing, threatening, or humiliating someone using digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, or online games.

Effects of Cyber Threats

  • Loss of personal and financial data
  • Damage to devices and systems
  • Emotional stress and anxiety
  • Loss of privacy and reputation
